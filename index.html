Making a webpage can be overwhelming, especially when you have to take design into consideration. There are millions of websites out there you can use as reference or you can use the information below!

Content

Best Practices

Less is more.

Consumers have finite focus and will only look at your site for so long.

Carefully select your content to only include what is absolutely needed.

Making intentional choices.

Pitfalls

Too much content is never good.

Lack of organization or thought to the placement of items.

Lack of flow or starting with the nonessential pieces.

Font

Best Practices

Less is more.

Fonts help a consumer focus on what they are looking at.

Use one font family throughout the entire site.

Sans-serif fonts are easiest on the eyes and easiest to read

Pitfalls

Using many fonts on the site.

Cursive or handwriting fonts seem like a good idea but are often hard to read.

Using bold or italics too often, or not enough.

Color

Best Practices

Less is more.

Color helps a consumer identify what is important and helps them focus throughout.

Pick a color family or intensity family to use throughout your site.

Use hex codes to represent unique colors.

Utilize color intentionally and with purpose.

Pitfalls

Using many colors throughout a site.

Using many colors while on the same view of a site.

Defaulting to the built-in colors, they are abrassive.

Coloring without purpose.

White Space

Best Practices

More is best.

White Space allows a consumer's eye to focus on the essential information.

Margins helps create white space around elements.

Padding helps create white space within an element and its border.

Pitfalls

Little White Space on the page.

Inconsistent spacing either in margins or padding.
